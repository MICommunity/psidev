Report

Achieved: Commencement of new development projects

(1) MIAPE. The MIAPE minimum reporting requirement represents a subset of the total information available from any proteomics experiment, containing just enough information to assess the provenance and relevance of a set of methods, results and conclusions. [cf. MIAME.]

(2) PSI-OM (and PSI-ML) + (3) PSI-Ont. The PSI Object Model and the associated ontology (PSI-Ont) are scoped to support the capture of any information (subject to some consistency constraints) from any proteomics experiment. The model and ontology will be as flexible as possible; we envision their use as far broader (or, equally, more narrow if you see what I mean) than just to encode the MIAPE requirement. [cf. MAGE-OM, MAGE-ML, MGED Ontology]

Together the minimum reporting requirement, data model, ontology and the various implementations provide a sound base upon which to build our knowledge; both of the many proteomes and of their biological contexts.

(4) Widespread vendor acceptance of the mzData subschema developed by Randy Julian of Eli Lilly and Kai Runte of EBI (plus initial input from Patrick Pedrioli of ISB, Seattle, and the PSI). Agreement that ASTM should 'own' the ontology work relating directly to mass spectrometry (in collaboration with PSI).

(5) Agreement to collaborate with the developers of MAGE, with the goal of providing FGE-OM -- the functional genomics object model -- featuring a common biological 'core' for release in September, and 'ome-specific modules to follow (PSI-OM will become one in time). Note that what people are calling MAGE 2 is the core and the transcriptomics module taken together (potentially slightly misleading).

Work begun in Carros

(1) ‘Principles’ document -- a statement of our guiding principles in all the different aspects of our development.
(2) MIAPE document (as defined above). First draft to come from Pierre-Alain Binz of SIB, presented at ASMS Nashville at the end of May.
(3) Modelling -- work has begun on PSI-OM -- details available at psidev.sf.net by midweek.
(4) Ontology -- work on the ontology will parallel that on the OM (crucial in collaborative development to avoid problems with ambiguity, crucial to the eventual use of the model also).
(5) Search engine metrics -- discussion document -- first draft to come from Sean Seymour of ABI, to be evolved by vendors and ISB.

Recruitment to the development process will be through offered resource, resulting in a self-selecting group.
This is already working well.

Silence = assent. Information will come from the website, but participation will be driven by those who wish to participate.

The future (before HUPO Beijing)

(1) Above items to reach a ‘mature stage’
(2) Branching OMG process (not done so as to hinder development though).
