Introduction and Organizers

Since its conception in April 2002, the HUPO Proteomics Standards Initiative has contributed to the development of community standards for proteomics in a collaborative and dynamic way, resulting in several published reports. The PSI Molecular Interaction standard was published in Nature Biotechnology in February 2004, and the PSI Mass Spectrometry standard is being implemented by a large number of instrument and analysis tool vendors. The strategy of focusing on well demarcated, feasible domains has been rewarded by significant progress in those focal areas within only 18 months.

However, much remains to be done:


Building on the successful development and quick adoption of the PSI MI Level 1 standard, the PSI: Molecular Interactions workgroup now need to exploit its potential by increasing inter-database collaboration to maximise database interoperability, and to promote it by contacting journals and funding agencies and asking them for support for the standard.

The PSI: Mass Spectrometry work group aims to extend its coverage beyond the peak list to encompass the full chain from the mass spectrometer to the identification list. Building on this set of standards (mzData and mzIdent), strategies for promotion and community acceptance need to be advanced.

Additionally, work must proceed on the next modular format, GelML; the ontology to support all the diverse formats from the PSI stable needs to be broadened and deepened; and we need to work intensively on the integration of technology- or domain-specific standards into an encompassing standard for proteomics data, similar to the work of, and in collaboration with the MGED consortium for the transcriptomics domain.

We will address all these issues and more at the PSI Spring Workshop, April 17th-20th, Siena, Italy. Participation in the meeting is free, thanks to generous sponsorship. Travel and accomodation are the responsibility of the attendee, but logistical help is available.
