Introduction:

Since its conception in April 2002, the HUPO Proteomics Standards Initiative has contributed to the development of community standards for proteomics in a collaborative and very dynamic way. As of December 2003, the PSI Molecular Interaction standard has been accepted for publication in Nature Biotechnology, and the PSI Mass Spectrometry standard is being finalised and already supported by a comprehensive set of tools. The initial strategy of focussing on limited, feasible domains has been rewarded by significant progress in the focal domains within only 18 months.

However, a lot remains to be done. Building on the successful development and quick adoption of the PSI MI Level 1 standard, we now need to exploit its potential by increasing inter-database collaboration to maximise database interoperability, and to promote it by contacting journals and funding agencies and asking them for support for the standard.
The PSI Mass Spectrometry work group aims to extend its coverage beyond the peak list to encompass the full chain from the mass spectrometer to the identification list. Building on this set of standards, strategies for its publication and acceptance need to be developed.
A strong demand for a standardisation of 2D gel data will for the first time be addressed by a specific work group.
And finally, we need to intensively work on the integration of technology- or domain-specific standards into an encompassing standard for proteomics data, similar to the work of, and in collaboration with the MGED consortium for the transcriptomics domain.
