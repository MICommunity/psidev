Mission Statement

The HUPO Proteomics Standards Initiative (PSI) defines community standards for data representation in proteomics to facilitate data comparision, exchange and verification. The PSI was founded at the HUPO meeting in Washington, April 28-29, 2002 (see Science 296, 827).

Work groups

Protein-Protein interaction

Several well-established public databases for protein-protein interaction data exist, including BIND , DIP , IntAct, MINT, and MIPS. However, the data were originally provided in many different, incompatible formats.

The PSI Molecular Interaction work group develops and maintains a common data standard that allows users to retrieve all relevant data from different sites and perform comparative analysis of different data sets in a consistent format. The standard comprises an XML format and detailed controlled vocabularies, and allows a detailed representation of a fully curated interaction record.

For a detailed documentation of the PSI Molecular Interaction standard, please see http://psidev.sourceforge.net/mi/rel25/ and the Nature Biotechnology paper. 

Mass spectrometry

Widely accepted public repositories for mass spectrometry based protein identifications do not exist at present and detailed experimental results are usually not available.

The PSI Mass Spectrometry work group develops a standard representation of experimental spectra in the context of the experimental setup and the analysed system. Such a format will support publications with detailed experimental results, and will allow data exchange and the comparision of related proteomics experiments beyond the local research team.

PSI-MS Format page.

General Proteomics Standards: Formats, minimum reporting requirements and ontologies

PSI-OM, PSI-ML, MIAPE, PSI-Ont

The generation and analysis of proteome data are now widespread. High-throughput approaches are commonplace, and techniques continue to increase in complexity. A standard way to represent proteomics data, and an agreed minimum required level of detail, are both urgently required to facilitate the analysis, dissemination and exchange of proteomics data.

Find out more at the HUPO PSI GPS Homepage 

Proteomics Informatics

Main Deliverable: analysisXML and associated documentation / tools

The PSI Proteomics Informatics standards group is a recent addition to the working groups of the Proteomics Standards Initiative.  Please look at the Proteomics Informatics Homepage for more details the activities of the group, including how to get involved.

Proteomics Informatics Group Charter

The PSI Proteomics Informatics (PSI-PI) standards group consists of academic, government and industry researchers, software developers, publishers and instrument manufacturers.  The goals of the group are:

to provide a set of minimal reporting requirements which augment the MIAPE reporting guidelines with respect to analysis of data derived from proteomics experiments; to provide vendor-neutral and standard formats for representing results of analyzing and processing experimental data; to foster adoption of the format by highlighting efforts made by vendors and individuals that utilize the format in their products.
