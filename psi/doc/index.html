Proteomics Standards Initiative

Mission Statement

The HUPO Proteomics Standards Initiative (PSI) defines community standards for data representation in proteomics to facilitate data comparision, exchange and verification.

The Proteomics Standards Initiative was founded at the HUPO meeting in Washington, April 28-29, 2002 (see Science 296, 827). PSI currenty develops standards for two key areas of proteomics, mass spectrometry and protein-protein interaction data, as well as  a standardised general proteomics format.

Work groups

Protein-Protein interaction

Several well-established databases for protein-protein interaction data exist, including BIND, DIP, MINT, Hybrigenics, and the MIPS interaction tables. However, their data are provided in many different formats and are not synchronised. In addition, many data producers offer only their data from their own web sites, again in different formats. The tedious work of combining the data from different data sources is performed over and over again.

The PSI Molecular Interaction work group develops a common data standard that will allow users to retrieve all relevant data from different sites and perform comparative analysis of different data sets much more easily than is currently possible. The standard defines a minimal data model that allows scientists to provide core data, but refer back to the original data source for full information, in particular for complex, fully curated entries.

As a long-term aim, the standard should allow a synchronisation of the core data between public protein interaction database providers, similar to the synchronisation of nucleotide data between EMBL, GenBank and DDBJ.

For a detailed documentation of the PSI Molecular Interaction standard, please see http://psidev.sf.net/mi/xml/doc/user/ and the Nature Biotechnology paper. 

Mass spectrometry

Currently, widely accepted public repositories for mass spectrometry based protein identifications do not exist; detailed experimental results are usually not available.

The PSI Mass Spectrometry work group develops a standard representation of experimental spectra in the context of the experimental setup and the analysed system. Such a format will allow to support publications with detailed experimental results, and will allow data exchange and the comparision of related proteomics experiments beyond the local research team.

General Proteomics Standards: Formats, minimum reporting requirements and ontologies

PSI-OM, PSI-ML, MIAPE, PSI-Ont

The generation and analysis of proteome data are now widespread. High-throughput approaches are commonplace, and techniques continue to increase in complexity. A standard way to represent proteomics data, and an agreed minimum required level of detail, are both urgently required to facilitate the analysis, dissemination and exchange of proteomics data.
