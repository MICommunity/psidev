About us

The HUPO PSI General Proteomics Standards workgroup (GPS) is tasked to provide standards with relevance to the whole of proteomics; we are developing solutions in several areas:

  • Minimum reporting requirements -- the evolving Minimum Information About a Proteomics Experiment (MIAPE) document contains guidelines on how to fully report a proteomics experiment. It is expected that this document will eventually go into print, and that the requirement be enforced by journals, compliant repositories and funders (cf. MIAME).
  • XML format for data exchange -- derived from the GPS proteomics workflow/data object model, PSI-OM, this markup language (known as PSI-ML) is designed to become the standard format for exchanging data between researchers, and submission to repositories or journals. We will also provide a generic SQL implementation, but this should only be viewed as a refernece implementation. The model and the exchange format will eventually form part of the emerging functional genomics model, which will be part developed by GPS in collaboration with the developers of the MAGE model for transcriptomics.
  • Ontologies -- these lists of clearly defined terms are crucial for the construction of unambiguously worded data files. The GPS will contribute terms to the MGED ontology (extended), under the 'psi' namespace. We will also coordinate with the Mass Spec (and Search Engines) working group, whose ontology terms will likely be owned by the American Society for Mass Spectrometry (ASMS).
  • Other activities -- We will soon produce a finished 'Design Principles' document, which summarises the principles we utiilise when evolving the design of both MIAPE and PSI-OM/ML. We are also coordinating the generation of a document examining the comparability of search engine metrics (e.g. Mascot versus SeQuest).

The MIAPE minimum reporting requirement

The context-sensitive nature of both transcriptome and proteome data necessitates the capture of a richer set of metadata (data about the data) than is required for basic genetic sequence, where usually knowing the organism of origin will suffice. The use of paper citations as proxies for actual metadata hinders the reassessment of data sets, and obstructs non–standard searching (e.g. by the order that different liquid chromatography columns were coupled). The requirements of the various journals also differ, so important detail may be lacking in some cases, or presented in an esoteric fashion.

There is then a need for public repositories that contain information from whole proteomics experiments; making explicit both where samples came from, and how analyses of them were performed. It is therefore appropriate to attempt to define the minimum set of information about a proteomics experiment that would be required by such a repository.

The first draft of this document will be presented at ASMS Nashville (May 23 - 27, 2004). The working draft, once available in CVS, will be linked from here...

The PSI Object Model

Data and metadata produced in different places may be in different formats, making comparison and exchange difficult. We therefore seek to develop a consensus object model and derived interchange format (XML Schema), to facilitate the development of effective search and analysis tools, simplifying both the dissemination and exchange of data. The development of the OM will be guided by our design principles, as laid out in the appropriate document, and will factor in the wisdom and experience of a wide range of concerned people, both in academia and industry. The XML Schema (PSI-ML) will be offered as a standard format for data exchange; a reference generic SQL implementation will also be offered, but as a guide only.

The object model (in various formats) will be viewable here, as will the markup and SQL implementations.

The PSI Ontology

The GPS ontology (notionally titled PSI-Ont) will take the form of an extension of the MGED ontology. The particulars of this developmental approach are still being worked out, details will be posted a.s.a.p.; however in the meantime a Word document will be made available here.
